Item #: 4743

Classification: Monkey Reporter

Special Containment Procedures: Item 4743 is to be contained in a standard primate containment unit within the Facility. The unit is to be outfitted with multiple CCTV cameras, motion detectors, and sound sensors to monitor Item 4743 at all times. Interaction with Item 4743 should be limited to authorized personnel only, and any contact must be approved by at least two Level 3 personnel.

Description: Item 4743 is a male monkey, with an unusual appearance. It has a pair of shade glasses over his eyes, and a bucket hat over his head. The forehead of Item 4743 is adorned with a two-color band, and it is commonly seen wearing a silver chain around its neck. The most peculiar accessory of Item 4743 is the LED gas mask it wears on its face. The reason for this gas mask is unknown at this time.

Personality: Item 4743 is characterized by its strong sense of independence. It shows a reluctance to ask for help, even in situations where it would be necessary. This trait often leads to humorous situations, such as Item 4743 trying to move a heavy piece of furniture by itself or stubbornly refusing to use a map when lost. Despite this, Item 4743 is a keen observer and reporter, always on the lookout for new stories to share. Its tenacious personality makes it a valuable asset to the Facility's research team.

Addendum: Item 4743 has been known to exhibit subservient behavior towards higher ranking staff members. Researchers are encouraged to observe this behavior and report in any potential changes in its personality.
