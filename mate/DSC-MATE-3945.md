Item #: DSC-MATE-3945

Object Class: Safe

Special Containment Procedures: DSC-MATE-3945 is to be contained in a standard humanoid containment chamber at Site-██. As DSC-MATE-3945 does not require sustenance, only periodic checks are required by Level 2 personnel. All communication with DSC-MATE-3945 must be conducted via video call. DSC-MATE-3945 is to remain cooperative at all times, any signs of aggressive behavior must be reported immediately to the Site Director.

Description: 

DSC-MATE-3945 is a male capuchin monkey standing at 30 cm tall and weighing 3 kg. DSC-MATE-3945 displays unique physical characteristics that differ from typical capuchin monkeys. The subject's eyes are always covered by shaded glasses and wears a blue bandana around its head. A jade earring decorates its right ear, and DSC-MATE-3945 is often seen with a red scarf wrapped around its neck. DSC-MATE-3945 possesses the ability to speak fluently in English, including slang and pop culture references. DSC-MATE-3945 has a unique sense of humor, and it uses its abilities to make others laugh by telling funny stories or doing silly impersonations. 

DSC-MATE-3945 has taken upon the role of a real estate agent as its primary occupation. It utilizes its intelligence and communication skills in its job, often closing deals to secure its commission. DSC-MATE-3945 has expressed a significant interest in humans and their way of life. While DSC-MATE-3945 seems to have a good understanding of human culture and society, it has not shown any desire to leave the Foundation’s containment. 

DSC-MATE-3945’s ability to communicate and its physical characteristics have allowed it to blend well into human society. The subject displays no signs of aggression and is generally cooperative with Foundation personnel. However, DSC-MATE-3945 tends to become irritated when it is not taken seriously or is made fun of for its appearance. It is advised to treat DSC-MATE-3945 like any other humanoid DSC subject. 

Addendum: DSC-MATE-3945 has shown to be resistant to all known strains of influenza viruses, and its antibodies could prove useful in developing a universal flu vaccine. Research on this topic is ongoing.
